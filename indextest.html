Hydrogen Storage Solution

H2GO Power has developed a safe method for hydrogen production and storage to provide power on the move. Our hydrogen acts as a fuel feedstock for fuel cells, clean high efficiency energy storage devices.

Material Design

We are a spin-out company from the Department of Chemistry, University of Cambridge (UK). Our core technology involves a novel hybrid smart material, which produces, stores and releases hydrogen gas in a controlled manner.

Platform Technology

Our unique solution allows for fuel cell manufacturers to deploy fuel cells into new markets such as portable off-grid electronics. The potential outcome of our technology includes a power supply with 5 times the energy capacity of current battery technology.

Technology

"We have developed a hybrid smart material that produces, stores and releases hydrogen in a controlled way"

The Hydrogen Sponge

Our core technology involves a novel porous catalytic material, which produces, stores and releases hydrogen in a controlled manner. Hydrogen is produced via the hydrolysis of chemical and metal hydrides at room temperature. Hydrogen is released through heating to moderate tuneable temperatures (< 100 °C), allowing easy on-off control.

Material Integration

The produced hydrogen can then be fed into a fuel cell which converts hydrogen's chemical energy into electrical power. We carry out system design and material integration allowing maximum effectiveness within each application area. Three feature approach

The smart material combines production, storage and controlled reaction into one system.

H2GO Power's patented technology involves a highly porous nano-particle-based smart material that combines production, storage and controlled release of hydrogen. This three feature approach allows for enhanced safety and complete fuel utilisation. The catalytic properties of the material allow for hydrogen production through the hydrolysis of metal and chemical hydrides at room temperature. The produced hydrogen is then trapped within the polymer framework, which acts as a storage medium. The stored hydrogen is released through the application of heat, with hydrogen release occurring at a specific tuneable temperature allowing for on-off control.
